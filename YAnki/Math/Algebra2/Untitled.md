### Factoring Binomials: Difference of Squares ($4x^2 - 1$)

---


The **Difference of Squares** formula is used to factor binomials in the form $\mathbf{a^2 - b^2}$.

**Formula:**
$$a^2 - b^2 = (a - b)(a + b)$$

#### Problem: Factor $4x^2 - 1$

1.  **Identify $a^2$ and $b^2$**:
    * The first term, $4x^2$, is $a^2$.
    * The second term, $1$, is $b^2$.

2.  **Find $a$ and $b$ (The Square Roots)**:
    * Find the square root of $a^2$: $\sqrt{4x^2} = \mathbf{2x}$ (This is $a$)
    * Find the square root of $b^2$: $\sqrt{1} = \mathbf{1}$ (This is $b$)

3.  **Apply the Formula**:
    * Substitute $a=2x$ and $b=1$ into the formula $(a - b)(a + b)$.
    $$(2x - 1)(2x + 1)$$

---

#### Final Factored Form
$$(2x - 1)(2x + 1)$$
